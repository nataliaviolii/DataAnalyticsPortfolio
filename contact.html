“Be the Leader You Wish You Had.” ~Simon Sinek

This quote has taught me a lot, and I believe it works both ways: "Be the junior you wish you had when you're a senior." I always imagine myself as a successful person in the future, able to help people in need. To get there, the journey will, of course, start from zero. For example, besides focusing on my studies, I also work as a waitress. I constantly ask myself: Would I love to have someone like me as a waitress if I were the owner of the restaurant? This question changes the way I work. Simply put, if I need to sweep the floor, I will do it to a good standard, regardless of whether my manager sees me or not. It's not just about completing the task, but about how I do it. I reflect on how I would feel as the restaurant owner and know I would appreciate a waitress who cleans the floor properly and serves wholeheartedly. At the end I will say to myself: Be the waitress I wish I had if I were the restaurant owner. I believe we reap what we sow, and by being faithful with little things, we will eventually be entrusted with bigger responsibilities. That’s why character matters more than skills or knowledge. Skills can be taught, but character is who they are.
